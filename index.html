Selected Publications [Full List]

Once-for-All: Skeleton-Guided SMT Solver Fuzzing with LLM-Synthesized Generators
Maolin Sun, Yibiao Yang, Yuming Zhou
Proceedings of the 2026 ACM International Conference on Architectural Support for Programming Languages and Operating Systems, 2026 (ASPLOS 2026, CCF-A) Contribution: Once4All is a novel LLM-assisted fuzzing framework for SMT solvers that synthesizes reusable term generators from documentation to efficiently ensure syntactic validity and semantic diversity, dramatically reducing computational overhead.

Isolating Compiler Faults via Multiple Pairs of Adversarial Compilation Configurations
Qingyang Li, Yibiao Yang, Maolin Sun, Jiangchang Wu, Qingkai Shi, Yuming Zhou
ACM Transactions on Software Engineering and Methodology, 2025 (ACM TOSEM, CCF-A) Contribution: MultiConf is a novel, highly accurate compiler fault localization approach that precisely pinpoints faulty files by leveraging multiple pairs of adversarial compilation configurations and a consolidated Spectrum-Based Fault Localization algorithm.

Using a Sledgehammer to Crack a Nut? Revisiting Automated Compiler Fault Isolation
Yibiao Yang, Qingyang Li, Maolin Sun, Jiangchang Wu, Yuming Zhou
Proceedings of the IEEE/ACM 48th International Conference on Software Engineering, 2026 (ICSE 2026, CCF-A) Contribution: This study contributes to a deeper understanding of practical compiler fault localization by demonstrating that the simple, developer-aligned BIC-based (BASIC) strategy significantly outperforms conventional Spectrum-Based Fault Localization (SBFL) techniques.

Validating SMT Rewriters via Rewrite Space Exploration Supported by Generative Equality Saturation
Maolin Sun, Yibiao Yang, Jiangchang Wu, Yuming Zhou
Proceedings of the ACM on Programming Languages, Volume 9, Issue OOPSLA2, 2025 (OOPSLA 2025, CCF-A) Repo Contribution: Aries is a novel validation technique that thoroughly explores the SMT solver's rewrite space by using mimetic mutation and deductive rewriting to identify critical bugs, particularly in the rewrite systems.

Isolating Compiler Faults through Differentiated Compilation Configurations
Yibiao Yang, Qingyang Li, Maolin Sun, Jing Yang, Jiangchang Wu, Yuming Zhou
IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering, 2025 (IEEE TSE, CCF-A) Contribution: ODFL is a simple yet effective compiler fault localization approach that substantially outperforms state-of-the-art techniques by leveraging differentiated compilation configurations (e.g., disabling fine-grained flags) to produce multiple pass/fail executions for more accurate Spectrum-Based Fault Localization.

Unveiling Compiler Faults via Attribute-Guided Compilation Space Exploration
Jiangchang Wu, Yibiao Yang, Maolin Sun, Yuming Zhou
Proceedings of the 2025 USENIX Conference on Usenix Annual Technical Conference, 2025 (USENIX ATC 2025, CCF-A) Repo Contribution: ATLAS is a novel attribute-guided compiler testing approach that strategically inserts C/C++ attributes into test programs, enabling a more flexible and comprehensive exploration of the compilation space to uncover bugs in compilers like GCC and LLVM.

Debugger Toolchain Validation via Cross-Level Debugging
Yibiao Yang, Maolin Sun, Jiangchang Wu, Qingyang Li, Yuming Zhou
Proceedings of the 2025 ACM International Conference on Architectural Support for Programming Languages and Operating Systems, 2025 (ASPLOS 2025, CCF-A) Repo Contribution: Devil is a novel debugger toolchain validation framework that introduces Cross-Level Debugging (CLD) to compare execution traces from the same executable at different debugging levels, overcoming limitations of prior approaches by establishing robust consistency relations.

ClozeMaster: Fuzzing Rust Compiler by Harnessing LLMs for Infilling Masked Real Programs
Hongyan Gao, Yibiao Yang, Maolin Sun, Jiangchang Wu, Yuming Zhou, Baowen Xu
Proceedings of the IEEE/ACM 47th International Conference on Software Engineering, 2025 (ICSE 2025, CCF-A) Repo Contribution: ClozeMaster is a novel Rust compiler fuzzing approach that harnesses the power of LLMs combined with a bracket-based masking and filling strategy (clozeMask) derived from historical bug reports to generate valid, semantic, and highly effective test cases.

SMT Solver Validation Empowered by Large Pre-trained Language Models
Maolin Sun, Yibiao Yang, Yang Wang, Ming Wen, Haoxiang Jia, Yuming Zhou
Proceedings of the 38th IEEE/ACM International Conference on Automated Software Engineering, 2023 (ASE 2023, CCF-A) Contribution: LasT is a practical LLM-based fuzzing tool that utilizes a novel retrain-finetune pipeline and data augmentation to effectively generate high-quality SMT formulas for comprehensively testing state-of-the-art SMT solvers.

Validating SMT Solvers via Skeleton Enumeration Empowered by Historical Bug-Triggering Inputs
Maolin Sun, Yibiao Yang, Ming Wen, Yongcong Wang, Yuming Zhou, Hai Jin
Proceedings of the 45th International Conference on Software Engineering, 2023 (ICSE 2023, CCF-A) Repo Contribution: HistFuzz is a novel SMT solver fuzzing approach that significantly improves bug-finding effectiveness by leveraging skeletons extracted from historical bug-triggering formulas and generating new test cases under the guidance of association rules.

Heterogeneous Testing for Coverage Profilers Empowered with Debugging Support
Yibiao Yang, Maolin Sun, Yang Wang, Qingyang Li, Ming Wen, Yuming Zhou
Proceedings of the 31st ACM Joint European Software Engineering Conference and Symposium on the Foundations of Software Engineering, 2023 (ESEC/FSE 2023, CCF-A) Repo Contribution: Decov is a novel heterogeneous testing technique that validates code coverage profilers by leveraging the hit counts monitored by debuggers as a distinct source, ensuring that the execution counts from profilers are consistent with the traces observed during debugging.

Automatic Self-Validation for Code Coverage Profilers
Yibiao Yang, Yanyan Jiang, Zhiqiang Zuo, Yang Wang, Hao Sun, Hongmin Lu, Yuming Zhou, Baowen Xu
2019 34th IEEE/ACM International Conference on Automated Software Engineering, 2019 (ASE 2019, CCF-A) Contribution: Cod is an automated self-validator for code coverage profilers that effectively uncovers bugs by employing a metamorphic relation which checks for consistency between the coverage statistics of a program and its mutated variant.

Hunting for Bugs in Code Coverage Tools via Randomized Differential Testing
Yibiao Yang, Yuming Zhou, Hao Sun, Zhendong Su, Zhiqiang Zuo, Lei Xu, Baowen Xu
2019 IEEE/ACM 41st International Conference on Software Engineering, 2019 (ICSE 2019, CCF-A) Contribution: It introduced a randomized differential testing approach to effectively uncover bugs in widely used C code coverage tools (like gcov and llvm-cov) by searching for and verifying inconsistencies in their coverage reports for randomly generated programs.

Effort-Aware Just-in-Time Defect Prediction: Simple Unsupervised Models Could Be Better Than Supervised Models
Yibiao Yang, Yuming Zhou, Jinping Liu, Yangyang Zhao, Hongmin Lu, Lei Xu, Baowen Xu, Hareton Leung
Proceedings of the 2016 24th ACM SIGSOFT International Symposium on Foundations of Software Engineering, 2016 (FSE 2016, CCF-A)Repo Contribution: Simple unsupervised models demonstrate superior or equivalent predictive power compared to state-of-the-art supervised methods in effort-aware Just-in-Time (JIT) defect prediction, highlighting their practical value.

An Empirical Study on Dependence Clusters for Effort-Aware Fault-Proneness Prediction
Yibiao Yang, Mark Harman, Jens Krinke, Syed Islam, Dave Binkley, Yuming Zhou, Baowen Xu
Proceedings of the 31st IEEE/ACM International Conference on Automated Software Engineering, 2016 (ASE 2016, CCF-A) Contribution: Dependence clustersâ€”sets of mutually inter-dependent program elementsâ€”are a significant indicator of software quality, as their analysis shows that larger, more important clusters are more fault-prone and enable a segmented prediction model that substantially improves the effectiveness of effort-aware fault-proneness prediction.

Are Slice-Based Cohesion Metrics Actually Useful in Effort-Aware Post-Release Fault-Proneness Prediction? An Empirical Study
Yibiao Yang, Yuming Zhou, Hongmin Lu, Lin Chen, Zhenyu Chen, Baowen Xu, Hareton Leung, Zhenyu Zhang
IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering, 2015 (IEEE TSE, CCF-A) Contribution: Slice-based cohesion metrics are valuable because they are complementary to common code and process metrics, leading to a statistically significant and practically important improvement in the effectiveness of effort-aware post-release fault-proneness prediction.
